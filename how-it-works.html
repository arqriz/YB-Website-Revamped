Accepted Items

What Can We Recycle?

Yellow Bin accepts all types of textiles and accessories. Here's everything you can drop off at any Yellow Bin near you.

Primary Category

Mixed Textiles & Clothes

We all have clothes in our wardrobes, on surfaces, in stuffed closets and drawers that we don't need or want anymore. Clothes and textiles can be passed on to someone in need, family members, or donation sites. Ideally they are recycled and made into new items such as padding for chairs and car seats, cleaning cloths, and industrial systems.

Items don't need to be clean or in perfect condition, we accept worn, torn, and faded textiles. The only things we cannot accept are wet or heavily soiled items. Items with holes, yellowing, pilling, or odors. We also exclude winter wear, Japanese kimonos, traditional attires that are incomplete sets and headscarves (Tudung).

Behind the Scenes

What Happens to Your Donation

Once it's in the bin, here's the transparent journey every item goes through.

01
Collected → Sent to YB HQ

Items are collected from all bin locations on a regular schedule and transported to our sorting centre in Johor Bahru.

02
Sorted by Grade

Manually sorted by our team. A part-time worker processes up to 1 ton of clothing per day.

Grade A — Wearable
Grade B — Worn but usable
Scrap — Damaged
03
Scrap → Circular Economy

Scrap and rejected textiles are sold to workshops as industrial rags or affordable used clothing, keeping every piece out of landfill.

04
Redistributed to Those in Need

Grade A and B clothing is redistributed to welfare homes, underprivileged communities, and charitable organisations across the region.
