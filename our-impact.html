Where It Goes

What Happens to Every Kilogram

Of every 100 kg collected, here's how we put it to use.

45%

Donated & Reused

Wearable clothing and accessories are redistributed to charities, welfare homes, and community organisations across the country.

40%

Recycled into Raw Fibre

Non-wearable textiles are shredded and processed into industrial fibres used for insulation, padding, and new textile manufacturing.

15%

Resold Commercially

Quality second-hand items are sold through partner resellers and op shops, extending the life cycle and generating revenue that funds our operations.

Zero to landfill — 100% diverted
The remaining 0% is handled through certified waste-to-energy partners.
Our Journey

Key Milestones

2020

Yellow Bin Founded

Started with 10 bins placed in Kuala Lumpur, with a mission to make textile recycling as easy as throwing something away.

2021

100,000 kg Milestone

Reached our first 100 tonne milestone, with bins now in 5 cities and partnerships with 8 major shopping centres.

2022

School & Community Program Launch

Launched our dedicated school and community drive programme, placing bins in 60+ schools and running quarterly collection events.

2023

1 Million KG Collected

Celebrated the 1 million kg milestone. By now, Yellow Bin had expanded to 30 cities with 250+ active collection points.

2024

Regional Expansion

Expanded operations to Australia, with 500+ bins now active across both countries. Formed partnerships with 15+ welfare and charity organisations.

2026 — Now

2 Million KG & Growing

We've now collected over 2 million kg of textiles. Our target is 5 million kg by 2028 — and with a growing community, we're well on track.
